1. Welcome and Overview

Welcome to the Ramachandra College Hackathon. This handbook is issued to all registered participants upon arrival at the college and serves as the official guide for the event.

The hackathon is designed to encourage innovation, problem solving, teamwork, and the development of practical solutions within a structured and competitive environment. Participants are expected to work on the assigned themes, follow the event guidelines, and present their solutions clearly and professionally.

The event focuses on practical AI-led innovation in the areas of Generative AI and Agentic AI. Teams are expected to build solutions that are meaningful, feasible, and clearly communicated within the event timeline.

4. Objective and Event Focus

This hackathon is intended to foster breakthrough innovation at the intersection of Generative AI and Agentic AI. Participants will be expected to:

  • Understand a problem statement quickly and clearly
  • Design a practical solution with a strong use of AI
  • Build a working prototype within the event duration
  • Communicate the solution effectively during reviews and final presentation

9. Submission Guidelines

Review 1

Participants present:

  • A clear understanding of the problem
  • The proposed solution direction
  • The initial system design or implementation plan
  • The intended use of AI in the solution

Review 2

Participants demonstrate:

  • Measurable progress in development
  • A working model, prototype, or core feature
  • The current technical approach
  • Any relevant tools, frameworks, or AI components

Final Submission

Participants submit:

  • The completed solution
  • A working demonstration of the product
  • Any supporting materials requested

If a repository, slide deck, or additional material is requested during the event, participants must submit it within the announced timeline.

Venue & Movement

Conducted within designated college areas. Key areas include: Main event hall, team working area, evaluation area, food/rest area, tech support, and organizing desk.

Participants are not allowed to leave the venue during the event. Exceptions only for emergencies with organizer permission.

18. Guidelines & 19. Rules

Team Guidelines

20. Jury Questions & 21. Pitch Structure

3-Min Pitch

1

Problem — clearly define the problem and why it matters

2

Solution — explain the AI-powered solution & features

3

Demo — show the prototype or a recorded demonstration

4

Impact/Future — explain scalability, usefulness, next steps

Round 1: Idea

  • What problem are you solving?
  • Why is it important?
  • Where is AI used?
  • Gen AI or Agentic AI?
  • Who are target users?

Round 2: Prototype

  • Demonstrate prototype
  • How does model function?
  • Tools/frameworks used?
  • Autonomous decisions?
  • Multi-agent workflows?
  • Agent communication?

Final Q&A

  • What makes it unique?
  • How scalable is it?
  • Real-world performance?
  • Future improvements?
  • Estimated impact?

Advanced questions may cover AI safety, hallucination handling, privacy, cost, and scalability.
